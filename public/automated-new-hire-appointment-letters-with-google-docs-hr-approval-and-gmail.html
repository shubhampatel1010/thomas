What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming and error-prone process of manually creating and sending appointment letters to new hires. HR teams typically spend 30-45 minutes per hire drafting documents, verifying details, getting approvals, and emailing finalized versions - multiplied across dozens or hundreds of hires annually.

The workflow automatically generates personalized appointment letters by merging candidate data with your Google Docs template. It then routes the draft to designated HR approvers via email or Slack notification. Only after approval does the system email the final letter to the new hire, with all communications logged for compliance.

How It Works

1. Trigger from HR system or form

The workflow initiates when a new hire record appears in your HRIS, ATS, or when a hiring manager submits a Google Form. Essential details like name, position, start date, and compensation are captured.

2. Document generation

The system creates a new Google Doc by merging the hire's details with your master appointment letter template. Dynamic fields populate all personalized information while maintaining consistent formatting and legal language.

3. Approval workflow

The draft document is shared with designated HR approvers via email with a review link. Approvers can suggest edits or approve with one click. The system waits for approval before proceeding.

4. Final delivery

Once approved, the system converts the document to PDF, attaches it to a professionally formatted email, and sends it to the new hire from your corporate email address. All documents are saved to a designated Google Drive folder with timestamps.

This automation benefits HR teams at growing companies that hire frequently (5+ employees monthly). It's particularly valuable for:
